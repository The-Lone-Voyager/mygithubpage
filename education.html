My Education

The university that I attend is Brescia University in Owensboro, KY. You can visit Brescia on the web by clicking here. Brescia is a small, catholic school located in Western Kentucky. I really enjoy the atmosphere here at Brescia. One of the reasons I chose to go here was because of the small size. There is a small student-to teacher ratio here at Brescia. This means that students can get to know their professors personally which I enjoy.

My major is computer science and my emphasis is in quantitative theory. This is essentially a math-centric computer science course of study. Janett Williams is my computer science professor and my advisor is the mathematics professor, Chris Tiahrt. I have a cumulative 3.9 total GPA and a 4.0 GPA in my major-only classes. Outside of class I also am a work-study student in the computer science department at Brescia. I work in the computer science lab on the fifth floor of the science building. This is a brand new lab which was just built in 2016. My duties include maintaining the lab, installing software, and helping students that need help with their computer science assignments, such as in programming. This is a great joy for me as I really like teaching programming to other students. My education here at Brescia has helped me to learn several programming languages such as C++, C#/.NET, HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I have also learned about operating systems and artificial intelligence during my studies here at Brescia. Overall, Brescia is a great school and I'm glad that I chose to go here for my education.
